<p><p>Imaging Scanning Monochromators are optical devices used to selectively transmit specific wavelengths of light while filtering out others. The market is divided into Single and Double Monochromators. Single Monochromators are more compact and cost-effective, ideal for applications requiring moderate resolution. In contrast, Double Monochromators provide enhanced wavelength selectivity and reduced stray light, making them suitable for high-resolution applications in research and industrial settings. Both types serve various industries, including spectroscopy, environmental monitoring, and biomedical research.</p></p>
