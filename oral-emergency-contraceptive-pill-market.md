<p><p>The oral emergency contraceptive pill market consists of two main types: progestin-only contraceptives and combined hormonal contraceptives. Progestin-only contraceptives, such as levonorgestrel, work primarily by preventing ovulation and thickening cervical mucus, making it difficult for sperm to reach the egg. Combined hormonal contraceptives contain both estrogen and progestin, inhibiting ovulation, altering the uterine lining, and preventing fertilization. Both types are effective when taken promptly after unprotected intercourse but differ in hormonal composition and mechanism of action.</p></p>
