<p><p>The commercial aircraft engine market comprises four main types: turboprop, turbofan, turboshaft, and piston engines. Turboprop engines combine a gas turbine engine with a propeller, ideal for regional flights. Turbofan engines, prevalent in jetliners, enhance fuel efficiency and noise reduction for commercial aviation. Turboshaft engines, commonly used in helicopters, convert turbine power to drive the rotor. Piston engines, resembling car engines, are utilized in smaller aircraft for general aviation, providing reliability and simplicity. Each type serves distinct operational needs.</p></p>
