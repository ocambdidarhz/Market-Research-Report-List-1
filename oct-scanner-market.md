<p><p>The OCT scanner market is primarily categorized into two types: 2D OCT scanners and 3D OCT scanners. 2D OCT scanners provide high-resolution cross-sectional images of tissues, allowing for effective diagnosis and monitoring of various eye conditions. In contrast, 3D OCT scanners offer volumetric imaging, enabling comprehensive visualization of retinal layers and structures in three dimensions. This enhances the accuracy of diagnoses and treatment planning. The choice between these types often depends on the clinical needs and technological advancements in ophthalmology.</p></p>
