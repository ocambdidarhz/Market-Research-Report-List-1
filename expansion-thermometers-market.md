<p><ul><li>Liquid Expansion Thermometer</li><li>Gas Expansion Thermometer</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Expansion thermometers are instruments that measure temperature by utilizing the principle of thermal expansion of a substance. The liquid expansion thermometer uses a liquid, typically mercury or alcohol, which expands in a calibrated glass tube as temperature rises. Conversely, gas expansion thermometers rely on gas confined within a bulb that expands and increases pressure with temperature changes. Both types are widely used in various applications, offering accurate temperature readings suited for industrial, laboratory, and environmental measurements.</p></p>
