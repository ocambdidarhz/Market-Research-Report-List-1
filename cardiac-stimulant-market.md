<p><p>The cardiac stimulant market is primarily divided into two types: cardiac glycosides and non-cardiac glycosides. Cardiac glycosides, such as digoxin, are derived from plants and used to enhance myocardial contractility and manage heart failure. Non-cardiac glycosides encompass a broader range of agents that stimulate the heart indirectly through mechanisms like improving blood flow or reducing workload. Both categories aim to support cardiovascular function, with applications in various heart-related conditions, influencing treatment strategies and patient outcomes.</p></p>
