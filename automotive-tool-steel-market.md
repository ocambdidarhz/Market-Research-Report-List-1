<p><p>The automotive tool steel market comprises three primary types: Carbon Tool Steel, Alloy Tool Steel, and High-Speed Tool Steel. Carbon Tool Steel, known for its hardness and edge retention, is commonly used for cutting tools. Alloy Tool Steel incorporates various elements to enhance strength and toughness, making it suitable for automotive dies and molds. High-Speed Tool Steel is designed for high-temperature strength and wear resistance, ideal for manufacturing cutting tools that operate at elevated speeds. Together, these types support diverse automotive applications.</p></p>
